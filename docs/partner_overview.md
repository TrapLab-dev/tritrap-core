# Partner Overview

TriTrap is relevant to partners who need controlled AI execution without adopting a public clone of the private deployment.

The public repository explains architecture, protocol posture, review boundaries, and maturity. Private operational proof and implementation mechanics remain controlled.

---

## Why Partners Care

Partners may care about TriTrap when AI execution needs:

- stronger authorization controls
- clearer separation of execution and verification
- evidence-aware review
- fail-closed behavior under uncertainty
- documented limitations and threat posture
- integration paths that do not require immediate disclosure of private deployment mechanics

TriTrap is most relevant when AI output needs to be governed, reviewed, or defended.

---

## What Partners Gain

A partner evaluation can focus on:

- whether the architecture matches their trust problem
- whether the protocol concepts fit their workflow
- whether execution modes match their latency and assurance needs
- whether arbitration behavior is acceptable
- whether evidence boundaries support review or audit

This can be evaluated conceptually before private deployment details are shared.

---

## What Partners Do Not Need Immediately

An initial partner discussion does not require:

- private proof archives
- node topology
- runtime paths
- service definitions
- key material
- operational commands
- verifier internals

Those materials belong in controlled due diligence, not broad introductory review.

---

## Conceptual Pilot Shape

A public-safe pilot discussion can be framed around:

1. selecting a bounded workload class
2. choosing an execution mode
3. defining permit scope
4. defining arbitration policy
5. defining evidence expectations
6. reviewing accepted and rejected synthetic proof examples

This keeps the pilot conversation practical without exposing private implementation details.

---

## Evaluation Questions

Partners should be able to ask:

- What workload needs governance?
- What level of assurance is required?
- What should happen when outputs diverge?
- What evidence must exist for review?
- What remains private during evaluation?

TriTrap is strongest when the answer is not just "run the model," but "govern the execution."
